Bound Exodus is an RPG inspired game that enhances combat through a dynamic execution system.
This project introduces a number of concepts such as runtime dynamism, procedural map generation, and heuristic based AI.

Post-Mortem

An ambitious project that growed both in codebase and scope quickly. The genre of the game combined many elements from traditional turn based rpg combat games as well as autobattlers to create a self playing system of move chains unique to each combat and movesets.

This section aims to dive into some of those lessons and how I've documented them to further expand on things that worked well, and what should have been done differently.

What Worked:
  • Project vision was deeply defined and documented in terms of background and overall desgin.
  • Ideas were developed through and through with consideration into how players would feel when playing and if gameplay made sense throughout the main loop.
  • The main incentive to play was very clear and compelling to explore what kind of combinations were possible.
  • Code was constantly being refactored and improved upon learning better ways to handle tasks. The importance of leaving as little technical debt as possible was my one of my biggest concerns and focuses to keep code extensible.
What Didn't Work:
  • The project throughout its lifetime went through a number of different iterations and changes. Shifting between different genres, themes, and platforms. With a limited amount of time to work on the project, the projects initial design was not fully fleshed out and defined, leading to a lot of time being spent on defining the identity of the game.
  • With the above in mind, the project with each iteration required some or maybe even all parts of the codebase to be rebuilt with new mechanics in mind. This style of development led to scenarios where I'd often find myself taking one step forward, then two steps back.
  • Project scope was always kept in mind. That being said, without a proper anchor or reference point to keep myself grounded to, it became difficult to discern what elements were necessary to the core experience as well as what was in desperate need of being worked on.
Future Changes:
  • Milestone-based development is not enough. There needs to be a significant weight placed on the production timeline and ensure that things are worked on in order of priority. This prevents workflow from being frantic and unorganized based on whatever feels right in the moment.
  • Documenting at least once a week on the biggest tasks accomplished as well as the biggest problems unearthed shouldn't be treated as a side project, but directly part of the production pipeline.
  • Accountability or feedback on scope should have been something I seeked out more often, letting the imagination run wild without direction can lead to quicker burnouts.
